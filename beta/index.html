TestFlight Beta Testing Instructions

Welcome Beta Testers!

Thank you for helping test WellCheck before public launch. Your feedback will directly shape the final product.

For Families with Children

What to Test

Setup (Parent)

  1. Add 3-5 trusted adults from your contacts
  2. IMPORTANT: Verify each contact receives intro text from WellCheck
  3. Set check-in interval (try 1 hour for testing)
  4. Set grace period (5-10 minutes)
  5. Add your full name and phone number in Settings
  6. Test Practice Mode with your child first

With Your Child (Ages 2-8)

1. Practice Mode Test:

  • Show them the emergency calling screen
  • Let them tap family photos
  • Watch the 5-second auto-advance to next contact
  • Note any confusion points

2. Emergency Mode Test:

  • Activate via "Open Emergency Calling"
  • Have child tap first contact
  • Watch if they wait for next contact (auto-appears)
  • See if they can navigate alone

3. Night Mode Test:

  • Activate before bedtime
  • Check if red clock is readable
  • Try having child exit (requires Face ID)
  • Verify they can't accidentally exit

What We're Looking For

  • ✅ Do kids instinctively tap the photos?
  • ✅ Do they understand to wait 5 seconds for next contact?
  • ✅ Is anything confusing or scary?
  • ✅ Can they navigate without help?
  • ⚠️ Any way a child could get stuck?

Known Limitations (Please Don't Report)

  • Lock screen: App won't launch from locked phone (iOS restriction)
  • Silent mode: No sound if phone is on silent (waiting on Apple approval)

For Adults/Elderly Users (No Kids)

What to Test

Setup

  1. Add your emergency contacts (family, friends, neighbors)
  2. IMPORTANT: Ask contacts if they received intro text from WellCheck
  3. Set realistic check-in interval (4-12 hours)
  4. Set first check-in time (morning routine)
  5. Set last check-in time (bedtime)

Check-In System

1. Daily Routine:

  • Tap "Check In" when you wake up
  • Verify notification arrives on time
  • Test grace period (intentionally miss one)
  • Confirm automated SMS sent to contacts

2. Emergency Activation:

  • Let timer expire completely
  • Wait through grace period
  • Ask your contacts: Did they receive emergency SMS?
  • Ask your contacts: Was the message clear?
  • Test emergency calling interface

3. Night Mode:

  • Activate before bed
  • Does it auto-activate at bedtime?
  • Can you exit easily in morning?
  • Red clock helpful or annoying?

What We're Looking For

  • ✅ Is check-in timing reliable?
  • ✅ Are notifications clear and timely?
  • ✅ Did contacts receive automated SMS alerts?
  • ✅ Would your contacts understand the SMS?
  • ✅ Is grace period duration right? (too short/long?)
  • ⚠️ Any false alarms?
  • ⚠️ Any way to miss real emergencies?

Known Limitations (Please Don't Report)

  • Lock screen: Must unlock phone to tap notification
  • Background limits: iOS may delay notifications if battery low

CRITICAL: Test Contact Opt-Out Notifications

This is essential to test:

1. Have a contact opt-out:

  • Ask one of your contacts to reply "STOP" to the WellCheck intro message

2. What should happen:

  • You should get a push notification
  • Contact should be marked as opted-out in app
  • You should see a warning about opted-out contact

3. Please report if:

  • ❌ You don't get notified when contact opts out
  • ❌ App doesn't show opted-out status
  • ❌ No warning appears

4. Re-opt-in test:

  • Have contact reply "START" to re-subscribe
  • Warning should disappear

Test Twilio Integration

Ask your emergency contacts to verify:

When You Add Them

  • ✅ Did they receive an intro text from WellCheck?
  • ✅ Was the message clear about what WellCheck is?
  • ✅ Did it include your name?

During Emergency Test

  • ✅ Did they receive emergency SMS automatically?
  • ✅ Did SMS include your name and phone number?
  • ✅ Was timing of SMS correct?
  • ✅ Did they receive second alert after 15 minutes?

Opt-Out Testing

  • ✅ Does "STOP" work to opt-out?
  • ✅ Do they get confirmation message?
  • ✅ Does "START" work to re-subscribe?

What to Report as Bugs

Critical Issues:

  • App crashes
  • Timer stops working
  • Notifications never arrive
  • Can't add contacts
  • Contacts don't receive Twilio intro message
  • No SMS alerts sent during emergency
  • Not notified when contact opts out
  • Can't make calls from emergency mode
  • Settings don't save
  • Features turn off without notification

Usability Issues:

  • Confusing wording
  • Buttons hard to tap
  • Text too small
  • Colors hard to see
  • 5-second wait too long/short
  • Feature doesn't work as expected

Enhancement Ideas:

  • Missing features you'd want
  • Better ways to do something
  • UI improvements

How to Provide Feedback

In TestFlight App

  1. Open TestFlight
  2. Select WellCheck
  3. Tap "Send Beta Feedback"
  4. Include screenshots if possible

Via Email

support@wellcheckapp.com

  • Subject: "TestFlight - [Your Issue]"
  • Include: iOS version, iPhone model, what you were doing

What to Include

  • What you expected to happen
  • What actually happened
  • Steps to reproduce
  • Screenshots/screen recordings
  • For SMS issues: Did contact receive message? Screenshot from their phone?

Testing Timeline

1 WEEK TESTING PERIOD

  • Days 1-2: Setup and verify Twilio messages work
  • Days 3-4: Daily check-ins and emergency flow
  • Days 5-7: Edge cases, opt-out testing, final feedback

Focus Areas

High Priority

  1. Twilio integration - Do intro and emergency SMS work?
  2. Opt-out notifications - Do you get alerted?
  3. Reliability - Does check-in system work consistently?
  4. Emergency flow - Would this work in a real emergency?

Medium Priority

  1. Usability - Can you/your child use it without confusion?
  2. Settings - Are all options clear?
  3. Notifications - Timing and clarity

Low Priority

  1. Visual design - Colors, fonts, spacing
  2. Performance - Speed, battery usage

FAQ

Q: How often should I test?
A: Daily check-ins for adults. 2-3 practice sessions with kids total.

Q: Should I use real phone numbers?
A: Yes - we need to test actual calling and Twilio SMS.

Q: Will my contacts get a lot of test messages?
A: Intro message once. Emergency SMS only during intentional tests. Ask them first!

Q: What if something goes wrong?
A: Report it! That's why we're testing. No real emergencies please.

Q: Can I invite others to test?
A: Not yet - we're keeping beta small. Share after public launch!

Q: Will my test data transfer to the real app?
A: No - you'll need to set up again at launch.
